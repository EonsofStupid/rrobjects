# Code of Conduct

RRF is published so it can be read, evaluated, and argued with. That only works if the
arguing is about the engine.

## The short version

**Be hard on the work. Be decent to the person.**

Tell us the benchmark is unfair. Tell us the architecture is wrong. Tell us the readiness gate
is a gimmick and here's why. That's the point of publishing it — a measurement nobody
challenged isn't evidence, it's a press release. A well-argued "this number is bullshit, here's
my repro" is the most valuable thing you can bring here.

None of that requires being cruel to anyone.

## Not welcome

- Harassment, personal attacks, or demeaning anyone — for who they are, what they know, or
  what they don't.
- Sexualized language or imagery; unwelcome attention of any kind.
- Publishing someone's private information.
- Deliberately wasting people's time: bad-faith argument, sealioning, or reopening a settled
  decision because you didn't like the answer.

## Technical disagreement is not misconduct

Being told you're wrong — bluntly, with evidence — is not a violation. Neither is a maintainer
closing your issue with a short reason. This project runs on evidence over vibes, and that
means direct answers. If a decision is made and recorded, it's made; relitigating it isn't a
contribution.

## Scope

Issues, discussions, the wiki, and any project space.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, close, or remove anything here, and block accounts, at their discretion.
Report a problem privately to the maintainers via
[a security advisory](https://github.com/EonsofStupid/rrf/security/advisories/new) if it's
sensitive, or open an issue if it isn't.

There's no committee and no appeals process. It's a small project; the standard is
"would a reasonable engineer want to keep reading this thread."
